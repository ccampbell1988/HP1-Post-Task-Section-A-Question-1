Overall Philosophy

This plan uses a structured approach to build skills, tactics, fitness, mindset, and team chemistry for top performance. It follows Hockey Canada’s Long-Term Player Development model, focusing on players in the later "Train to Train" or "Train to Compete" stages. Key priorities include player health, recovery, long-term growth, and strong habits. The goal is to lead, develop, and promote positive hockey experiences.

Overall Philosophy

This plan uses a structured approach to build skills, tactics, fitness, mindset, and team chemistry for top performance. It follows Hockey Canada’s Long-Term Player Development model, focusing on players in the later "Train to Train" or "Train to Compete" stages. Key priorities include player health, recovery, long-term growth, and strong habits. The goal is to lead, develop, and promote positive hockey experiences.

Yearly Plan: September - March

1

SEPTEMBER: Foundation & Formation

(Pre-Season/Early Season) - "Forming" Stage

Overall Goals & Objectives:

  • Establish team identity, culture (vision, core values, norms), and expectations aligned with the "Canadian Way Principles".
  • Finalize player evaluations and team selection based on a comprehensive assessment of Talent, Character, Competitiveness, and Expectations.
  • Develop a strong aerobic base and foundational strength, guided by S&C coach collaboration.
  • Introduce core team systems (offensive, defensive, special teams).
  • Foster initial team cohesion ("Forming" stage) and open communication channels, adapting to Gen Z athlete needs.
  • Introduce the concept of "High Performance Habits" and Personal Development Plans (PDPs).

Player Selection / Tryouts (Weeks 1-3):

Process: Minimum of four prep skates/skill sessions prior to formal evaluations. Formal evaluations to include a minimum of one skills session, one small-area games session, and one formal game.

Focus: Technical, Tactical (Hockey Sense), Physical, Mental/Character.

Show Full Tryout & Other September Details...

Technical Focus During Tryouts: Skating (agility, speed, transitions), Puck Skills (control, protection, creativity), Passing (accuracy, reception), Shooting (quick release, accuracy), Defensive Skills (angling, stick positioning, 1v1) – using drills from "Player Assessment Plan #1".

Tactical (Hockey Sense) Focus During Tryouts: Decision-making in SAGs and scrimmages, reading play, positional understanding.

Physical Focus During Tryouts: On-ice compete level, work rate. Off-ice baseline fitness testing (e.g., Standing Long Jump, 40m sprint, Pull-ups, Beep Test, Pro-Agility).

Mental/Character Focus During Tryouts: Coachability, response to adversity, work ethic, team-first attitude, "Canadian Way Principles" (e.g., win battles, stop on pucks, manage pucks).

Evaluation Tools: Hockey Canada Player Evaluation Sheet (1-4 scale), detailed KEPs for drills.

Communication (Tryouts): Clear criteria communicated pre-tryout. Post-tryout, individual meetings to discuss roles, expectations, and PDP initiation. For those not selected, provide constructive feedback where possible.

Practices / Games: Practices: 4-5 on-ice sessions/week (90-120 min). High volume, moderate-high intensity. Emphasis on skill repetition (Wooden's 8 Laws of Learning), conditioning, and introducing basic tactical concepts (forecheck, DZC, NZ regroup, basic PP/PK structures). Integrate "Practice with Purpose and Speed" and "Figure It Out (F.I.O)" philosophies. Games: 2-3 exhibition games. Focus on implementing basic systems, evaluating game application, and building chemistry.

Technical / Tactical / Team Play Development: Technical: Focus on foundational skills from National Team Technical Curriculum. Drills from "Player Assessment Plan #1" are key. Tactical: Introduction to team systems. Initial video sessions to introduce concepts. Team Play: Begin establishing on-ice communication. Use drills to develop initial linemate chemistry. Introduce "Championship Habits".

Physical Performance: Off-Ice (3-4 sessions/week - S&C Coach Led): Focus: Aerobic base, foundational strength, core, mobility. Communication: Head coach updates S&C coach on player fatigue/soreness and any on-ice physical deficiencies observed. On-Ice: Conditioning integrated into drills. Nutrition & Hydration: Education on fueling and recovery.

Mental Performance: Introduction to mental skills for Gen Z: Goal Setting (SMART Process Goals), Focus/Concentration (WIN), Resilience (growth mindset), Communication (clear, brief, consistent). Establish pre-practice/pre-game routines.

Team Development: Forming Stage: Team meetings to define team vision, core values, rules, expectations, and season goals. Activities: Icebreakers, defining team identity, introduction of leadership group. Introduce Personal Development Plans (PDPs).

2

OCTOBER: System Implementation & Early Competition

(Regular Season) - "Storming/Norming" Stage

Overall Goals & Objectives:

  • Refine and solidify team systems in competitive situations.
  • Improve consistency in execution of technical skills and "Canadian Way Habits."
  • Develop game-specific fitness and maintain strength, managing load with S&C coach.
  • Enhance team cohesion ("Storming" into "Norming" stage) and problem-solving.
  • Begin tracking performance metrics (technical, tactical, physical, mental) via PDPs and other tools.
Show Full October Details...

Player Evaluation: Ongoing evaluation in practices/games against established criteria. Individual meetings to provide feedback (PDP review) and adjust roles.

Practices / Games: Practices: 3-4 on-ice sessions/week (75-90 min). Reduced volume, increased intensity, game-specific scenarios. Focus on tactical execution, special teams, correcting errors (video-informed). Games: 1-2 league games/week. Focus on applying systems, competing, adapting.

Technical / Tactical / Team Play Development: Technical: Refinement of core skills at speed and under pressure. Position-specific skill development. Tactical: Deeper understanding of systems. Introduce variations (PP breakouts, PK pressure). Focus on transitions. Heavy use of video analysis (team and individual clips). Team Play: Improving on-ice communication, support, anticipation. Executing set plays. Reinforce "Championship Habits."

Physical Performance: Off-Ice (2-3 sessions/week - S&C Coach Led): Maintain strength/power, speed/agility, core/mobility. Adjust volume based on game load. Emphasize recovery strategies (sleep, nutrition, hydration). Communication: Regular updates to S&C coach on game intensity, player fatigue. On-Ice: Game-intensity drills, SAGs. Monitor player load.

Mental Performance: Focus/Concentration: Techniques for full game duration. Imagery/Visualization: Introduce basic visualization (successful plays). Emotional Control: Strategies for managing pressure (breathing techniques, mental anchors). Confidence: Build self-confidence and trust in teammates. Reinforce understanding of roles and their value ("4 Wheel Drive Jeep" concept). Gen Z: Continue brief, clear communication. Focus on process in feedback.

Team Development: Storming/Norming: Address conflicts constructively. Establish clear norms. Activities: Regular team meetings (discuss progress, challenges, opponents), player-led activities (e.g., warm-ups), line/pair meetings, cooperation tasks. Reinforce team culture and values. Review PDP goals. Encourage player leadership.

3

NOVEMBER: Refinement & Adaptation

(Regular Season) - "Norming/Performing" Stage

Overall Goals & Objectives:

  • Achieve higher consistency in team performance and "Canadian Way Habits."
  • Adapt tactical approaches based on opponents.
  • Maintain physical fitness and minimize injury risk through S&C collaboration.
  • Strengthen mental toughness, resilience, and emotional control.
  • Further develop leadership within the team.
Show Full November Details...

Player Evaluation: Continuous performance monitoring. PDP check-ins.

Practices / Games: Practices: 3 on-ice sessions/week (75-90 min). High-intensity, game-like drills, special teams refinement, address weaknesses. Introduce new wrinkles/adjustments to systems. Games: 1-2 league games/week. Focus on executing game plans, in-game adjustments.

Technical / Tactical / Team Play Development: Technical: Mastery of core skills under pressure. Advanced techniques (deceptive shooting, puck protection). Tactical: Advanced understanding of systems, adjustments. Develop "Hockey IQ" (reading play, anticipation). Strategies for key situations (5v6, 6v5). Increased video for pre-scouting. Team Play: Seamless execution. Stronger unit cohesion. Effective communication and problem-solving on bench/ice.

Physical Performance: Off-Ice (2-3 sessions/week - S&C Coach Led): Power development (plyos, Olympic lifts if appropriate), maintain strength, speed, agility, recovery. Individualized adjustments. Communication: Discuss specific player needs (e.g., "getting knocked off pucks too easily") with S&C coach for targeted interventions. On-Ice: Drills emphasizing quickness, reaction, sustained high-intensity. Proactive injury prevention.

Mental Performance: Emotional Control: Composure in high-stakes situations. Self-Talk: Develop effective positive self-talk and thought-stopping. Coping Strategies: For setbacks and adversity (embrace failure, learn from it – "Black Box Thinking" concept). Motivation: Maintain focus through the grind. Gen Z: Personalize and customize approaches to build confidence. Help them discover meaning in their roles.

Team Development: Norming/Performing: Solidify roles and norms. Enhance trust and collaboration. Activities: Leadership development workshops/discussions (e.g., what makes a good leader, player-led standards meetings), review/adjust team goals, activities for morale. Encourage peer accountability.

4

DECEMBER: Sustaining Performance & Managing Load

(Regular Season)

Overall Goals & Objectives:

  • Maintain high performance leading into holiday break.
  • Effectively manage player workload and fatigue (critical communication with S&C coach).
  • Fine-tune strategies. Reinforce mental skills and team unity.
Show Full December Details...

Player Evaluation: Performance reviews and PDP check-ins before break.

Practices / Games: Practices: 2-3 on-ice sessions/week (60-75 min). Reduced volume, maintain intensity. Sharp execution, special teams, tactical refreshers. More recovery-focused towards break. Games: 1-2 league games/week. Focus on consistent execution, managing energy.

Technical / Tactical / Team Play Development: Technical: Maintain skill sharpness. Efficiency of movement/decision-making. Tactical: Review/reinforce systems. Analyze opponent trends. Team Play: Maintain strong communication/cohesion. Role clarity.

Physical Performance: Off-Ice (2 sessions/week - S&C Coach Led): Maintenance of strength/power. Increased emphasis on active recovery, mobility, flexibility. Monitor for overtraining. Guidance on staying active during break. On-Ice: Shorter, sharper practices. Nutrition: Education on managing diet during holidays.

Mental Performance: Strategies for staying focused/motivated during distractions. Revisit goals. Stress management. Gratitude/reflection. Gen Z: Provide reassurance and trust. Reinforce belonging cues.

Team Development: Performing Stage: Team activities to celebrate first half, build camaraderie. Clear communication for break/return. Focus on "Championship Habits".

5

JANUARY: Ramping Up & Playoff Preparation

(Regular Season) - "Performing" Stage

Overall Goals & Objectives:

  • Regain peak condition. Elevate intensity/focus for playoff push.
  • Refine all aspects of team play to high execution level. Build momentum.
  • Solidify playoff positioning.
Show Full January Details...

Player Evaluation: N/A for selection. Focus on optimizing current roster and individual contributions to team success. PDPs are key.

Practices / Games: Practices: 3-4 on-ice sessions/week (75-90 min). Increased intensity/competitiveness. Focus on high-pressure situations, special teams excellence, playoff-style hockey. Games: 1-2 critical league games/week. Focus on winning key matchups.

Technical / Tactical / Team Play Development: Technical: Skills sharp, consistent, precise under pressure. "Game-breaking" skills. Tactical: Mastery of systems, rapid in-game adjustments. Detailed pre-scouting, specific game plans. Practicing scenarios (protecting lead, tying game). Team Play: Peak cohesion. Exceptional communication, trust, anticipation. Flawless execution of complex plays.

Physical Performance: Off-Ice (2-3 sessions/week - S&C Coach Led): Maintain peak power/strength. Emphasis on speed, agility, explosive movements. Diligent recovery. Communication: S&C coach adapts plan for "Preparation for Playoffs" phase. On-Ice: High-tempo practices simulating playoff intensity. Injury Management: Quick, effective management.

Mental Performance: Develop "Playoff Mindset": heightened focus, resilience, determination. Advanced visualization for critical moments. Strategies for increased pressure. Composure/discipline in emotionally charged games. "Deliberate Practice" of mental skills.

Team Development: Performing Stage: Reinforce team identity and belief. Leadership stepping up. Strong sense of urgency/commitment. Final push team-building/motivational sessions. Connect to team vision and values.

6

FEBRUARY: Peaking & Playoff Mindset

(Late Regular Season / Early Playoffs) - Peak "Performing"

Overall Goals & Objectives:

  • Achieve peak team performance. Secure best playoff seeding or begin playoffs.
  • Fine-tune all elements for playoff intensity. Ensure all players physically/mentally prepared.
  • Foster unwavering belief.
Show Full February Details...

Player Evaluation: Focus on role execution and contribution to team success.

Practices / Games: Practices: 2-3 on-ice sessions/week (60-75 min). High intensity, shorter duration. Opponent prep, special teams, sharpness. "Quality over quantity". Games: Crucial late-season or early playoff rounds. Focus on precise execution, adapting, winning.

Technical / Tactical / Team Play Development: Technical: Flawless execution under extreme pressure. Minimize errors. Tactical: Deep understanding of opponent tendencies, tailored game plans. Execute multiple strategies, adjust on the fly. Mastery of situational play. Use of timeouts effectively. Team Play: Ultimate synergy/trust. Unselfish play. Resilience.

Physical Performance: Off-Ice (1-2 sessions/week - S&C Coach Led): Primarily recovery, mobility, maintaining power/speed. Very short, sharp workouts. Individualized. Strict adherence to nutrition, hydration, sleep. On-Ice: Short, intense, focused practices. Prioritize rest/recovery.

Mental Performance: Peak mental toughness: perform under max pressure, bounce back. Strong belief/confidence. Effective arousal regulation. "One shift, one period, one game at a time" mentality. Gen Z: Continued positive reinforcement, focus on their contributions and value.

Team Development: Peak Performing: Strong leadership (coach and player). Reinforce the "why." Positive, supportive, determined environment. "The time is now - not the past".

7

MARCH: Championship Phase

(Playoffs) - "Performing/Adjourning"

Overall Goals & Objectives:

  • Win championships. Perform at absolute peak.
  • Demonstrate resilience, adaptability, composure under highest pressure. Leave everything on the ice.
Show Full March Details...

Player Evaluation: N/A for selection.

Practices / Games: Practices: 1-2 on-ice sessions/week (45-60 min) or as needed. Highly opponent-specific. Tactical walkthroughs, special teams adjustments, maintain sharpness/energy. Emphasis on rest/recovery. Games: Playoff series. Focus: Winning.

Technical / Tactical / Team Play Development: Technical: Consistent, error-free execution. Making right play at right time. Tactical: Brilliant execution of game plans. Exploit opponent weaknesses. Swift, effective in-game adjustments. Team Play: Unified, synchronized effort. Sacrifice for team. Exceptional resilience.

Physical Performance: Off-Ice (S&C Coach Led): Almost entirely recovery: active recovery, stretching, massage, nutrition, hydration, sleep. Brief, light activation workouts. Manage fatigue/injuries. On-Ice: Conserve energy, maintain peak readiness.

Mental Performance: Unshakeable confidence/belief. Laser focus. Thrive under pressure. Emotional control. Draw on all mental skills training. For Gen Z, reinforce their journey and growth.

Team Development: Performing/Adjourning: Culmination of team building. Strong, unified leadership. Every player embraces role. Post-Season (Adjourning): Debrief, celebrate successes (team & individual using PDPs), plan for off-season. Manage emotions of season ending.

Key Evaluation & Tracking Tools

Assessment & Planning

  • Hockey Canada Player Assessment Plans & Tracking Sheet: Standardized drills and rating scales for tryouts and in-season technical/tactical assessment.
  • Personal Development Plan (PDP): Comprehensive tool for goal setting (SMARTER) and tracking across Hockey, Academics, S&C, Intangibles/Leadership, Mental Performance, Life.
  • Self-Evaluation Radar: Visual tool for players to self-assess across Mental, Tactical, Physical, and Emotional sectors.

Performance & Management

  • S&C Communication Loop & Monthly Workload Plan: Essential for managing physical load and optimizing off-ice training.
  • Video Analysis: For technical correction, tactical understanding, and providing player feedback.
  • Game Cards: For in-game tracking of lines, special teams, and key observations.

Development Frameworks

  • Team Building Framework: Using Tuckman's stages to guide activities for social and task cohesion throughout the season.
  • Mental Skills Implementation Strategies: Specific techniques for goal setting, emotional control, imagery, self-talk, and deliberate practice, tailored for Gen Z.

Culture & Habits

  • Leadership Principles: Framework for coaches to build connection, motivate, partner with, and care for players to foster leadership.
  • Habit Formation & Tracking: Identifying and reinforcing key on-ice ("Canadian Way") and off-ice (preparation, recovery) habits.

Important Considerations

Individualization

Adapt approaches for individual player needs, leveraging PDPs. Recognize that each athlete learns and develops differently, requiring personalized coaching and support to maximize their potential.

Flexibility

This plan is a template; adjust based on team progress, injuries, schedule, and unforeseen circumstances. A rigid plan can hinder development; adaptability is key to navigating a long season successfully.

Communication

Open, consistent, clear, brief, and positive communication is paramount between all stakeholders (players, coaches, parents, S&C staff). Effective communication builds trust and ensures everyone is aligned.

Recovery

A critical component for sustained performance and injury prevention. Emphasize sleep, nutrition, hydration, and active recovery strategies throughout the season.

Enjoyment & Positive Environment

Foster a supportive, respectful, and enjoyable atmosphere to enhance motivation, learning, and team cohesion, especially crucial for Gen Z athletes. Positive experiences contribute to long-term love of the game.

LTPD Alignment

Ensure all training, competition, and recovery demands are appropriate for the athletes' developmental stage according to Hockey Canada's Long-Term Player Development model. This promotes holistic development and reduces burnout.

Progress Tracking (Sample)

Skill Development

Tracking improvements in core technical skills (skating, puck handling, shooting, passing) through targeted drills and game application.

Physical Conditioning

Monitoring strength, speed, agility, and endurance gains based on S&C program adherence and on-ice performance metrics.

Tactical Understanding

Assessing growth in game sense, positional play, system comprehension, and decision-making in various game situations.

Mental Fortitude

Observing development in focus, resilience, coachability, leadership, and overall contribution to team culture and performance.
